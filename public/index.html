About

When?

29 March 2017, doors opening at 8:30am. This weekday 9 to 5 conference means no additional child-care costs or giving up your weekend.

Where?

The event will be taking place at the Cardiff Bierkeller, so convenient in case you have to pop back to the office.

Low cost

Tickets are £15 including breakfast, lunch, and refreshments. Add a donation to support our chosen charity.

Breadth is the new black

Our bearded wonders are giving talks that everyone can get something out of. Learn about infrastructure, software craftmanship, data visualisation, and front-end coding. Come learn a little about a lot!

Do something good

Attend to have a good time and raise funds that will support CALM, a male suicide awareness and prevention charity. Your support will help them offer vital emergency counselling services.

Have some fun

As well as presentations in the main arena during the day, there’ll be lots of side things going on throughout the day and into the evening including Nerd Bring’n’Buy, game tournaments, and more

PS Beards are not an entry requirement! Whilst Bring your own Beard (BYOB) is nice, this is a day open to anyone who wants to learn and support a good cause.

It’s (still) okay to be pragmatic!

Deciding to adopt an Agile way of working is great! Being precious about the agile framework you decide to use is not. For one reason or another, since adopting Agile, we’ve become a community of obsessive practitioners who seem unable to take a pragmatic approach to making project or process decisions.
Never has such a sense of entitlement been seen in project teams than in those who have recently converted to Agile. ‘Agile experts’ have constantly told teams that to get any value, you take an all or nothing approach towards implementation such as Scrum. Belittling all previous process ideas, Agile culture can sometimes be toxic and can turn the development team against the business if not properly facilitated.
In this session, we’ll explore how an Agile transition is never an overnight job and that to succeed in any type of project delivery, pragmatism is still a vital ingredient. Having a vision to be better with Agile is crucial, but so is an understanding that it will take time, it will be disruptive, and it should be pragmatic.

Thinking Securely

With more and more of us working remotely or on the road, it is important to think about security precautions. Here we will discuss things like where and how to work in public places. Also, looking at things you can do to help protect the information on your devices from prying eyes, both from a technical and non-technical perspective.jo

Static Code Analysis: What the Tool Vendors Don’t Want You to Know…

This session will present a whirlwind tour of what so-called “static analysis” can do for you coding. There are lots of tool, most of which make outlandish claims about their abilities to make your code better, find bugs, wash whiter, and make you a better person. We’ll touch on what the tools can really do, their limitations and what’s wrong with almost all of today’s programming languages.

Making a Men’s Health Data Viz…

I would like to discuss the feasibility of generating a data visualisation that would help inspire us all (men & women) to change any possible bad habits we have in a more positive direction. I am envisioning a visualisation that would allows us to explore the effects our habits are having on our life expectancy and would allow us to identify what change would bring the greatest benefit. Some parts of this will be coded in advance using R.

Jekyll and Hub

GitHub has built increasing support for the Jekyll static blog generator system into it’s platform. Starting with a brief introduction of the benefits of a free blogging and web hosting platform, the session will also be a walk through of how to set up a GitHub pages blog from scratch, including a brief step into liquid tags and YAML.
